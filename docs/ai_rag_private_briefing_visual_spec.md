# AI/RAG Private Briefing Visual Spec

## Purpose

Define the visual and information design for a private 5-slide AI/RAG reference-data freshness briefing.

## Use Status

- Private draft.
- Not public.
- Not buyer-facing proof.
- For internal review or 1:1 exploratory explanation only.

## Overall Design Principles

- Plain and technical.
- Low-hype.
- Source-aware.
- Limitation-forward.
- No broad commercial claims.
- No emergency, market, or model-quality framing.

## Slide 1: What World Pulse Reviews

Main message:

World Pulse reviews how selected public reality records relate to later activity in structured reference systems.

Layout:

- Left: one-sentence explanation.
- Right: simple three-step diagram.
- Footer: use status line, small and quiet.

Key text:

"World Pulse compares selected public reality records with activity in structured reference systems to review reference-data freshness windows."

Visual element:

Reality event -> Candidate reference pages -> Pageview window

What not to include:

- No product pitch.
- No large numeric score.
- No broad claim about AI/RAG systems.

## Slide 2: Why This May Matter for AI/RAG

Main message:

AI/RAG systems rely on reference systems, and reference systems may show uneven activity across domains and event types.

Layout:

- Top: compact problem framing.
- Middle: two-column contrast.
- Bottom: small boundary box.

Key text:

- "Reference systems update unevenly across domains and event types."
- "World Pulse reviews selected event-page windows."

Visual element:

Two-column box:

| Reference-system question | World Pulse review |
| --- | --- |
| Which pages are linked to the event? | Candidate page set |
| Did pageview movement appear in the window? | Daily pageview window |
| Is the example clear enough to discuss? | Internal scoring |

Boundary box:

"Exploratory review, not diagnosis."

Main message:

The top samples can be discussed safely if evidence and limits stay together.

Layout:

- Top: short evidence table.
- Right or bottom: visible limitations box.

Suggested evidence table:

| Sample | Strongest movement | Candidate pages | Safe reading |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| May 2024 G5 | `Geomagnetic_storm` ratio 131.41 | Compact category set | Clear daily-window movement |

Limitations box:

- No causality claim.
- No real-time attention claim.
- No prediction claim.
- No emergency or action relevance.
- No market relevance.
- No model-quality diagnosis.

What not to include:

- No standalone ratio chart without context.
- No claim that pageview movement proves response.
- No model evaluation language.

## Chart/Table Rules

- Avoid complex charts.
- Prefer small tables.
- Show ratios only with context.
- Do not imply causality.
- Always show limitations near evidence.
- Keep page names and event dates close to the values.

## Tone Rules

- No hype.
- No urgency.
- No broad product claims.
- No "AI systems are failing" framing.
- No "the world did not notice" framing.
- Use source-linked, retrospective language.
