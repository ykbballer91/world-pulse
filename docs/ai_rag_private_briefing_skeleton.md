# AI/RAG Reference-Data Freshness Private Briefing Skeleton

## Purpose

Create a private briefing structure for explaining how World Pulse evaluates reference-data freshness using source-recorded reality events and structured reference-system activity.

This is a skeleton for internal review. It is not public material and is not a finished external document.

## One-Sentence Summary

World Pulse compares selected public reality records with later activity in structured reference systems to evaluate where reference-data freshness may require review.

## What World Pulse Measures

- Selected source-recorded reality events.
- Candidate reference pages linked to those events.
- Pageview windows around event dates.
- Relative movement around selected event windows.
- Source-linked internal examples.

## What World Pulse Does Not Measure

- Real-time public attention.
- Causality.
- Prediction.
- Emergency or action relevance.
- Market relevance.
- Model deficiency.

## Why AI/RAG Teams Might Care

AI/RAG systems depend on reference systems.

Reference systems update unevenly. Some domains may produce clearer reference-system movement after selected events than others.

World Pulse can help review candidate freshness windows. It does not diagnose model quality directly, and it does not claim that a model is deficient because a reference page moved or did not move.

## Current Best Category: Historical Space Weather

Historical Space Weather is currently the best internal category because:

- Candidate pages are cleaner than earthquake candidates.
- Candidate pages are less location-dependent.
- Source-recorded historical events are available.
- Multiple internal examples score 10 or above under the internal criteria.
- Pageview-window movement is easier to explain in selected samples.

This does not mean Space Weather is the final product category. It means it is currently the clearest internal test bed for reference-data freshness review.

## Appendix

### Scoring Criteria Summary

Each internal example is scored from 0 to 2 across six dimensions:

- Reality event clarity.
- Candidate page existence.
- Pageview movement clarity.
- Lag measurability.
- Political/religious neutrality.
- Misinterpretation issue.

Total score ranges:

- 10-12: potentially presentable after review.
- 7-9: internal use only.
- 0-6: not suitable.
