# Historical Space Weather Sample Audit

## Purpose

Use selected historical Space Weather events to test whether Reality-Reflection examples can be evaluated faster than waiting for accumulated NOAA snapshots.

## Why Historical Samples

- Current NOAA local coverage is short.
- Waiting 30, 60, and 90 days is reliable but slow.
- Historical public events can test the method faster.

## Rules

- Events must be source-verifiable.
- Do not claim prediction.
- Do not claim real-time public awareness.
- Do not claim infrastructure effect.
- Do not claim causality from pageviews.
- Treat results as internal sample audit.

## Candidate Event Selection

Start with 3 to 5 events only. Each event must include:

- `event_date`
- `event_time_utc` if known
- `event_type`
- `event_label`
- `source_url` or `source_note`
- candidate reference pages
- `reason_for_selection`

## What Can Be Claimed

- Candidate reference pages can be checked.
- Pageview windows can be compared around event dates.
- Some pages may show measurable movement.

## What Cannot Be Claimed

- No public awareness claim.
- No causality claim.
- No emergency or action relevance.
- No market relevance.
