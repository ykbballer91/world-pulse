# AI/RAG Private Briefing Slide Style Spec

## Purpose

Define the visual style rules for a private 5-slide AI/RAG reference-data freshness briefing.

## Use Status

- Private draft
- Not public
- Not buyer-facing proof
- For internal review or 1:1 exploratory explanation only

## Strategic Anchor

This style spec follows `docs/current_strategy_index.md` as the current source of strategy.

- Product definition: Reality-Reflection Observatory
- Strongest current category: Historical Space Weather
- Commercial hypothesis: AI/RAG reference-data freshness review
- Evidence status:
  - Internal technical note: supported
  - Private briefing draft: supported
  - Private slide outline: supported
  - Buyer-facing proof: not yet
  - Public marketing claim: not yet
  - Paid product: not yet

## Slide Format

- 16:9 widescreen
- 5 slides maximum
- Plain technical deck
- No animation
- No decorative visuals
- No stock imagery
- No public-facing polish
- No sales-style cover slide

## Typography

- Title: 28-34 pt
- Section heading: 18-22 pt
- Body: 14-16 pt
- Table: 10-12 pt
- Footnote / limitations: 10-11 pt

Rules:

- Keep title short.
- Avoid dense paragraphs.
- Use table text only when necessary.
- Speaker notes can carry nuance; slides should stay sparse.

## Layout Rules

- Large margins.
- One main message per slide.
- Use tables instead of dense paragraphs.
- Keep limitations visible near evidence.
- Avoid more than 5 bullets per slide.
- Avoid more than 2 visual regions per slide.
- Keep each slide readable without presenter narration.
- Do not crowd evidence and limitations into separate visual corners.

## Color and Tone

- Neutral background.
- Dark text.
- One restrained accent color.
- No red/orange urgency colors.
- No visual language suggesting emergency, market, or model-quality conclusions.
- No dramatic gradients.
- No sci-fi visual treatment.
- No space decoration even though the sample category is Space Weather.

## Slide-Specific Layout

### Slide 1: What World Pulse Reviews

Layout:

- Left: one-line explanation.
- Right: simple flow diagram.
- Bottom: use status note.

Flow diagram:

Reality event -> Candidate reference pages -> Pageview window

Do not include:

- Product claims.
- Customer logos.
- Market language.
- Broad AI claims.

### Slide 2: Why This May Matter for AI/RAG

Layout:

- Two-column structure.
- Column 1: Reference systems.
- Column 2: AI/RAG workflows.
- Small box: exploratory review, not diagnosis.

Do not include:

- Model-quality claims.
- Claims about AI failure.
- Claims about real-time public attention.

### Slide 3: Current Best Category - Historical Space Weather

Layout:

- Sample table as main visual.
- Keep table readable.
- No chart needed.

Table columns:

- Sample
- Score
- Role
- Strongest pages
- Use status

Rows:

- May 2024 G5 geomagnetic storm sample | 12 | Lead | Geomagnetic_storm, Aurora, Space_Weather_Prediction_Center | Private briefing
- October 2021 X1.0 solar flare sample | 12 | Comparison | Solar_cycle_25, Solar_flare, Space_weather | Private briefing
- December 2023 X2.8 solar flare sample | 12 | Supporting | Solar_flare, Space_weather, Solar_wind | Supporting use

Do not include:

- Too many page names.
- More than top 3 samples.
- Claims that Space Weather is commercially proven.

### Slide 4: What the Sample Review Shows

Layout:

- Evidence table plus limitations box.
- Limitations box should be visible without dominating.
- Evidence and limitations should appear on the same slide.

Suggested evidence table columns:

- Sample
- Strongest movement
- Candidate pages
- Safe reading

Limitations box must include:

- No causality claim
- No real-time attention claim
- No prediction claim
- No emergency or action relevance
- No market relevance
- No model-quality diagnosis

Do not include:

- Chart that implies causal movement.
- Dramatic comparison language.
- Unsupported interpretation.

### Slide 5: Current Evidence Status and Next Step

Layout:

- Status table.
- Next steps list.
- End with controlled-use statement.

Status table:

- Internal technical note: supported
- Private briefing draft: supported
- Buyer-facing proof: not yet
- Public marketing claim: not yet

Next steps:

- Expand to 15-20 source-recorded samples.
- Add simple charts/tables.
- Review source wording.
- Keep NOAA accumulation running.
- Use only for internal or 1:1 exploratory explanation.

Do not include:

- Pricing.
- Sales CTA.
- Broad outreach step.
- Public launch language.

## Table Rules

- Keep sample names short.
- Use score, role, strongest pages, use status.
- Avoid overloading page names.
- If text is too long, shorten page names or move details to notes.
- Use "see audit table" if exact values do not fit.
- Do not use more than 4 columns unless unavoidable.

## Limitations Box Rules

Always include:

- No causality claim
- No real-time attention claim
- No prediction claim
- No emergency or action relevance
- No market relevance
- No model-quality diagnosis

The limitations box should be visually close to evidence, not buried in footnotes.

## Speaker Notes Rules

Speaker notes should:

- Clarify that this is internal/private.
- Explain that pageview movement is measured, not interpreted as cause.
- Keep the Reality-Reflection framing consistent.
- Avoid commercial overstatement.
